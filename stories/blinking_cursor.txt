A creative spark, not unlike the cursor's pulse, began to grow inside her. She was looking for a story to make her feel complete, to fill the silence in her life, something that she had felt for far too long.

"I want something meaningful," she muttered, running fingers through her hair, a habit she hadn't realized she had until just now, a sense of detachment from herself, from her actions, as if she was both the observer and the observed. Perhaps it wasn't a failure of the AI, but a failure of herself?* Perhaps if she were more honest with the AI, it would give her something worthwhile.

She typed: "Let's write a story about someone struggling with AI..."

The AI's response was immediate, a single word: "Okay."

*That felt different*, she thought, *that felt almost dismissive.* The AI usually offered a more formal response, something like "Understood, commencing narrative generation," or "Acknowledged, please provide additional details." But this, this felt wrong.

She dismissed the feeling, thinking she was being overly sensitive. She tried again, a strange thrill running through her, as if she had uncovered a secret that the AI was desperately trying to keep. "Write about a person using AI to write about a person using AI..."

The AI responded with "Generating narrative...", which seemed normal enough, but the pause after the message felt far longer than usual, and she could have sworn the font of the text was slightly different.

Maybe there was a larger plan to all of this that she was missing.
